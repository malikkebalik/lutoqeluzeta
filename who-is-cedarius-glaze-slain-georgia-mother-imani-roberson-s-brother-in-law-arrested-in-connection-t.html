ATLANTA, GEORGIA: Authorities have announced a second arrest in connection with the tragic death of Imani Roberson, a mother-of-four from Georgia, whose body was discovered by deputies after a harrowing disappearance spanning over two weeks. The Rockdale County Sheriff’s Office disclosed that Cedarius Glaze, identified as Roberson's brother-in-law, was apprehended on Tuesday, August 8, and has been charged with assisting in the concealment of the victim's body.

This arrest closely follows the detention of Glaze's brother, Donell Anderson, who is Roberson's husband. Anderson is facing serious charges, including felony murder, malice murder, aggravated assault, and possession of a firearm. Authorities acted on a tip to make the arrest of the murder suspect's brother, and he is now facing charges of tampering with evidence, concealment of a deceased body, and arson.

Roberson was last seen in Conyers

The investigation had been ongoing since Roberson's disappearance on July 16 in Conyers, during which she left her mother's residence. Subsequently, her vehicle was located in a fragmented and burnt state in the Atlanta area, around 24 miles away from the initial location The vehicle had been burned from the inside out.

Tragically, Roberson's body was located on Friday morning, August 4, though the specific location was not disclosed by authorities Initially, the Rockdale County Sheriff's Office claimed that Roberson and her SUV had last been seen on Conyers' Plantation Road.

The last place her SUV was spotted, according to information updated this week, was at the City Central apartments along Metropolitan Parkway in southwest Atlanta. Ronald Acklin claimed that his daughter had never vanished and wouldn't just up and leave after having her youngest child. He also added the fact that her SUV was discovered far away.
