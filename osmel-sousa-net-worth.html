Osmel Sousa Net Worth

July 07, 2024
blog

Osmel Sousa, born Osmel Ricardo Lazaro Cipriano Sousa Mansilla, is a Cuban entrepreneur who gained fame as the president of the Miss Venezuela Organization. He is popularly known as “The Tsar of Beauty” for his successful reign, leading Venezuela to victory in various beauty pageants worldwide. As of early 2017, his net worth is estimated to be a substantial $20 million, primarily amassed from his fruitful tenure with the Miss Venezuela Organization.

Sousa, hailing from Rodas, Cienfuegos, Cuba, spent his formative years under the care of his grandmother before relocating to Maracaibo, Venezuela. Following his high school education, he enrolled in Horacio Peterson’s acting school, yet later shifted his focus from acting to pursue a career in advertising. He embarked on his professional journey as an art designer and advertising draftsman for television programs and commercials, securing positions with prominent networks such as VTV and RCTV. His early contributions to the advertising industry not only provided him with income but also paved the way for his involvement with the Miss Venezuela Organization.

In 1969, while serving a publicity firm, Sousa became associated with the Miss Venezuela Organization, eventually dedicating more of his time to the enterprise. He assumed the role of an advisor to selected contestants and even undertook the design of their nightgowns for the competition. His discerning eye for beauty propelled the contestants he mentored to clinch numerous titles.

In 1981, Sousa ascended to the presidency of the Miss Venezuela Organization, steering the company to unparalleled success, marked by the production of multiple pageant winners. Over his three-decade tenure, he played an instrumental role in securing triumphs in competitions such as Miss Universe, Miss World, Miss International, and Miss Earth, significantly augmenting his net worth.

In 2007, Sousa participated as a judge in Univision’s Hispanic beauty contest, Nuestra Belleza Latina, alongside Lupita Jones and Carlos Calderon. Despite his achievements, Sousa encountered criticism during his presidency, facing backlash for expressing preferences against Venezuelan women with African features and for allegedly endorsing and advocating for contestants to undergo plastic surgery.

Presently, Sousa continues to hold the position of president at the Miss Venezuela Organization, while maintaining a private personal life with minimal media disclosure.
