Who Is Hallie Jackson?

Hallie Jackson is the Senior Washington Correspondent for NBC News. She was born on April 29, 1984, in Yardley, Pennsylvania, to the couple David Jackson and Heidi Jackson. Hallie attended Pennsbury High School and scored brilliant grades. She graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from John Hopkins University in 2006.

She started her career with WBOC-TV in Salisbury and Dover in 2006, soon after her graduation. She then worked for WFSB-TV in Connecticut as a journalist. Later in 2008, she worked as a public commentator for Hearst Corporation. She was then hired by NBC News in 2014. She covered the campaign of Ted Cruz (II) for the Republican presidential nomination, after which she was promoted to anchor the news show "MSNBC Life." In 2017 she was named MSNBC's Chief White House Correspondent. She has covered numerous stories so far and is one of the richest journalists in the country.
