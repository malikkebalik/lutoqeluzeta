Miriam Shor-Professional Life, Careers

Talking about her career, Miriam Shor began her acting career in 1998 by appearing onstage as Yitzhak in Hedwig and the Angry Inch. Later, she made her appearance in the short-lived ABC sitcom Then Came You in 2020.

Moreover, she played the supporting roles in the Harold Ramis comedy remake Bedazzled (2000) and the 2001 cinematization of Hedwig and the Angry Inch as Yitzhak. She also played the NBC short-lived comedy series, Inside Schwartz, opposite Breckin Meyer and Maggie Lawson from 2001-2002.

She also made a cameo appearance in Shortbus as Cheryl in 2006. In the same year, she featured in the ABC television comedy series Big Day opposite Marla Sokoloff and Wendie Malick. However, the series was called off after one season.

Likewise, she played a supporting role in the drama The Cake Eaters. Miriam appeared as Janet Thompson in the 1970s-set primetime CBS drama series Swingtown but later show was canceled due to mixed reception and low ratings.

Similarly, she also appeared in Law & Order: Criminal Intent, Bored to Death, and Royal Pains, and had a recurring role on FX legal drama Damages as ‘Carrie Parsons.

Adding more, she portrayed the role of Cynthia O’Neal in the biographical drama film Maestro directed by Bradley Cooper in 2023. In the same year, she made her appearance as as Recorder Vim in Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3, and in the drama film American Fiction.
