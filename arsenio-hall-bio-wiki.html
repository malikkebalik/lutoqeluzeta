Who is Arsenio Hall?

Arsenio Hall is a well-recognized American actor, comedian, talk show host, writer, and producer.

Hall is famed for hosting the late-night talk show, The Arsenio Hall Show from 1989 until 1994 and again from 2013 to 2014.

Moreover, he has featured in Martial Law, Coming to America(1988), Coming 2 America(2021), and Harlem Nights(1989).

Arsenio Hall- Age, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity, Education

Arsenio Hall was born on 12 February 1956. At present, he is 65 years young star. Likewise, his birthplace is Cleveland, Ohio, the USA.

Arsenio is the son of his father, Fred Hall whereas his mother is Annie hall. Moreover, he has not mentioned his sibling’s information in the public yet.

Talking about his nationality, Hall holds an American identity whereas his ethnicity is African-American.

Regarding education, the actor graduated from Warrensville Heights High School in Warrenville, Ohio in 1973. Later, Arsenio Hall studied at John F. Kennedy High School. Hall has also studied at Ohio University and Kent State University.

Arsenio Hall- Professional Life, Careers

Arsenio Hall first moved to Chicago then to Los Angeles in order to start his career in comedy. He was soon featured in a couple of appearances on Soul Train.

Later, he was the announcer for Alan Thicke during the short-lived talk show, Thicke of the Night.

Moreover, Hall also featured on the five weeks of short-lived NBC game Match Game-Hollywood Squares Hour from 1983 to 1984.

Arsenio Hall was also the original voice of Winston Zeddemore in the animated series, The Real Ghostbusters from 1986 to 1987.

In 1987, the star hosted the show and his stint proved immensely popular, leading to his being offered his own show in syndication.

Arsenio Hall also hosted a nationally syndicated late-night talk show, The Arsenio Hall Show. Hall also had a rivalry with Jay Leno after the latter was named host of The Tonight Show, during which Arsenio said that he would “kick Jay’s ass” in ratings.

Arsenio Hall then became a contestant on the fifth edition of The Celebrity Apprentice which premiered on 19 February 2012. He represented his charity, the Magic Johnson Foundation. For winning The Celebrity Apprentice, Hall won the $250,000 grand prize for his charity.
